## 3) Formal abstract

This project investigates whether a wave-based relational perspective can provide a disciplined methodological entry point toward a possible bridge between quantum structure and spacetime-related effective organization. The present stage does not propose a completed theory of unification. Instead, it introduces a pair-based H3 framework designed to test whether a controlled and interpretable intermediate signal can be identified without relying on broad speculative claims.

The current setup is based on a fixed-logic architecture and has passed a mixed-like sensitivity test. In addition, a D1/D2 divergence has been demonstrated, indicating that the observed behavior does not collapse into a practically trivial null-pattern reading. At the same time, the present evidence remains explicitly bounded. The main result is not that a full spacetime mechanism has been established, but that the detectable effect appears to reside in weighted relational structure rather than in topology or in derived distance geometry.

Taken together, these findings support a cautious methodological interpretation: the framework yields bounded but readable structural evidence and helps narrow where a relevant signal may lie, while also clarifying where it likely does not. The project therefore positions itself as a stepwise, testable research program that emphasizes reproducibility, controlled intermediate claims, and scientific restraint.
