# PRE_READABLE_ORGANIZATION_CANDIDATE_MARKERS_NOTE

## First candidate marker families for pre-readable organization

**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Status:** internal candidate-marker note

---

## 1. Purpose
This note fixes the first candidate marker families for the pre-readable-organization block.

Its purpose is to answer the next narrowing question:

> **Which organization-side markers should be considered first when testing whether deeper bridge-relevant structure is already active before readable articulation becomes strong enough to count as formed?**

This is a candidate-marker note, not a result note.

---

## 2. Why candidate markers are needed
The pre-readable block is now:

- conceptually strong,
- methodologically separated,
- but operationally still open.

To move beyond concept, the block now needs at least one first marker set on the organization side.

Without candidate markers, the block remains unable to test its core question in a disciplined way.

So this note defines the first marker families to be treated as plausible organization-side candidates.

---

## 3. Core selection principle
A pre-readable organization marker should satisfy the following logic:

- it should be earlier than later readability/form markers
- it should not simply be a morphology/readability surrogate
- it should not require fully formed readable structure to become visible
- it should plausibly reflect structured organization rather than mere raw overlap

A marker that fails these conditions should not be treated as a first organization-side candidate.

---

## 4. First candidate family A — Structured support coherence

### Working idea
The first candidate family should ask whether local or mesoscopic relation environments are already internally organized in a way that exceeds raw overlap, even before later readable form becomes clear.

### Why it is plausible
This family is plausible because pre-readable organization should likely appear first as:

- non-random internal consistency
- structured local agreement
- support-side coherence
- or reduction of internal conflict

This sits naturally between:
- overlap-only
and
- later readable form

### Why it belongs first
This family is the closest organization-side analogue to the strongest current compatibility-side intuition:
- that something may begin to “hang together” before it becomes readable as full form

### Main risk
It may collapse into:
- smoothed overlap
- or weak early versions of later readability

So anti-collapse discipline will be important.

### Current status
**Tier 1 organization-side candidate family**

---

## 5. First candidate family B — Persistent local structural asymmetry

### Working idea
The second candidate family should ask whether a local or mesoscopic structure already shows a stable, non-random asymmetry pattern that suggests organization, even while later readability remains weak.

### Why it is plausible
Pre-readable organization may show up not only as coherence, but also as:

- stable directional bias
- persistent imbalance pattern
- non-random local articulation
- or asymmetry that is too structured to count as noise, but not yet readable enough to count as formed morphology

This is a plausible early-organization line.

### Why it is second, not first
This family is useful, but it is more interpretation-sensitive than support coherence.
It is therefore a good second candidate family, but not the cleanest first one.

### Main risk
It may drift into:
- vague irregularity
- family-specific quirks
- or a pretext for overreading weak structure

### Current status
**Tier 2 organization-side candidate family**

---

## 6. First candidate family C — Local organization persistence under weak perturbation

### Working idea
The third candidate family should ask whether a local organization pattern remains detectably non-random under small perturbation, even before later readable structure becomes strong.

### Why it is plausible
A pre-readable organization marker may show itself as:

- weak but non-accidental persistence
- not yet full stabilization
- but already more than fragile noise

This family tries to capture:
- early organizational resilience without requiring late support closure

### Why it is not first
This family is attractive, but it sits closer to stabilization-side concerns and therefore carries more leakage risk.

It may still be useful later, but should not currently outrank the cleaner earlier candidates.

### Main risk
It may collapse into:
- hidden stabilization logic
- or a weak precursor to rigidity rather than a distinct organization marker

### Current status
**Tier 2 to Tier 3 border candidate family**

---

## 7. Recommended first-pass priority order
The current best first-pass organization-side priority order is:

### Priority 1
**Structured support coherence**

### Priority 2
**Persistent local structural asymmetry**

### Priority 3
**Local organization persistence under weak perturbation**

This is the current recommended order.

---

## 8. What should not yet be used as organization-side markers
The following should currently be avoided as first organization-side markers:

- full readability or morphology-like measures
- late bridge-shape measures
- direct grid-like form surrogates
- broad “interesting structure” judgments
- signals that require readable regime structure to already be obvious

These are too late or too vague.

---

## 9. Working first candidate recommendation
For the very first operational narrowing step, the best current recommendation is:

> **Start with one structured-support-coherence marker as the first organization-side candidate.**

This is the cleanest first move because it is:

- closest to the organization/readability gap
- earlier than full readable structure
- and less likely to be mistaken for late morphology than the alternatives

This should be the first candidate family taken forward.

---

## 10. Relationship to the compatibility block
These organization-side candidates are related to the compatibility block, but not identical to it.

### Compatibility candidates ask
- which early signals may function as supportability selectors before stabilization?

### Pre-readable organization candidates ask
- which early signals may already indicate structured organization before later readability becomes strong?

So the overlap in spirit is real, but the target is different.

This distinction should remain explicit.
