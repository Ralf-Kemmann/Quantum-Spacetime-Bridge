# DLBSR_DECISION_RULE_NOTE

## Compact decision rule for DLBSR-class in the first compatibility pass

**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Status:** internal decision-rule note

---

## 1. Purpose
This note gives the compact first-pass decision rule for:

> **DLBSR-class = Downstream Local Bridge-Support Response class**

Its purpose is to reduce the downstream target to one explicit operational rule that can be applied consistently in the first compatibility pass.

This is a decision-rule note, not a result note.

---

## 2. Working role of DLBSR-class
DLBSR-class is the first narrow downstream outcome target for the compatibility first pass.

It is intended to be:

- later than Candidate A and Candidate B
- narrower than full readability or morphology
- distinct from the overlap-only baseline
- distinct from the later-stabilization boundary

So DLBSR-class should answer only:

> **Has later local bridge-support become materially present or not?**

That is its whole role.

---

## 3. Compact binary decision rule
### Decision rule
Assign:

- **DLBSR-positive**  
  if the chosen narrow downstream local bridge-support indicator exceeds a pre-declared support-positive threshold strongly enough to count as later local support materially present

- **DLBSR-negative**  
  otherwise

This is the compact first-pass rule.

---

## 4. Minimal verbal form
A short usable version is:

> **DLBSR-positive = later local support materially present.  
> DLBSR-negative = later local support not materially present.**

This is the preferred compact wording.

---

## 5. What “materially present” means in the first pass
For the first pass, “materially present” should be read conservatively.

It means:

- more than descriptive nonzero signal
- more than weak or noisy local response
- more than support-possible
- enough later local bridge-support response to count as support-present

So the decision rule is intentionally stricter than:
- “some later response exists”

That is important.

---

## 6. What this rule must not collapse into
The decision rule must not become:

- a full readability decision
- a morphology decision
- a direct rigidity copy
- a vague “interesting later structure” label
- a threshold adjusted after candidate behavior is seen

If that happens, DLBSR-class loses its first-pass usefulness.

---

## 7. Required first-pass discipline
For the first pass, the DLBSR decision rule must be:

- binary
- pre-declared
- identical for Candidate A and Candidate B
- recorded explicitly in the first-pass result
- and not redefined mid-pass

This is mandatory.

---

## 8. Safe summary sentence
A useful internal sentence is:

> The first-pass DLBSR rule is binary and conservative: positive means later local bridge-support is materially present, negative means it is not.

A shorter version is:

> **DLBSR = local downstream support: yes or no.**

---

## 9. Bottom line
The compact first-pass DLBSR decision rule is:

> **Assign DLBSR-positive only when later local bridge-support is materially present according to a pre-declared narrow support criterion; otherwise assign DLBSR-negative.**

That is the current compact DLBSR-class decision rule.
