# PRE_READABLE_ORGANIZATION_CANDIDATE_A1_STRUCTURED_SUPPORT_COHERENCE_NOTE

## Candidate Marker A1: structured support coherence as the first organization-side marker

**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Status:** internal candidate-marker note  
**Marker ID:** PRO-A1-SSC

---

## 1. Purpose
This note defines the first concrete organization-side marker for the pre-readable-organization block:

> **Candidate Marker A1 = Structured Support Coherence**

Its purpose is to take the first Tier-1 organization-side family and make it concrete enough for later first-pass comparison work.

This note does **not** claim that A1 is already a validated pre-readable marker.

It only fixes:
- what A1 is supposed to mean,
- where it is supposed to sit,
- why it is plausible,
- and how it should be distinguished from overlap-only and later readability.

This is a candidate-marker definition note, not a result note.

---

## 2. One-sentence meaning
Candidate Marker A1 is intended to capture whether a local or mesoscopic relation environment already shows internally structured support coherence strong enough to count as early organization, even while later readable articulation remains weak or absent.

---

## 3. Why A1 is the right first organization-side marker
A1 is the correct first marker because it is the cleanest operational expression of the pre-readable hypothesis.

The pre-readable block asks:
- can structure already be there before it is clearly readable?

The best first place to look is therefore not:
- full morphology,
- global shape,
- or broad readability,

but rather:
- whether a local environment already hangs together in a non-random support-structured way.

That is exactly what A1 is meant to test.

---

## 4. Structural role in the current chain
A1 is intended to sit:

- after raw overlap
- before later readable form
- before broad morphology/readability
- and without requiring full stabilization closure

Working role in the chain:

`overlap -> early structured support coherence (A1) -> later readable articulation boundary -> broader readability/form`

This makes A1 an organization-side marker, not a readability marker.

---

## 5. Earliest computation point
### Intended earliest computation point
A1 should be computable from local or mesoscopic relational structure before later readable form becomes clearly established.

That means:
- before later morphology/readability measures are used
- before full readable bridge articulation is already obvious
- and ideally from local support organization alone plus local neighborhood structure

### Timing judgment
**Target status:** clearly pre-readable

### Main timing danger
If A1 requires:
- already readable bridge form
- late morphology proxies
- or downstream readability labels

then it has drifted too late and no longer counts as a pre-readable marker.

---

## 6. Required inputs
A1 should ideally use only early organization-side inputs such as:

- local overlap structure
- local neighborhood relations
- local support agreement
- local conflict / fragmentation pattern
- possibly sign or phase-sector consistency if available and still early enough

A1 should **not** require:
- late readability labels
- morphology/readability scores
- full bridge-shape descriptors
- or already obvious regime-level articulation

That is essential for keeping A1 on the pre-readable side.

---

## 7. Provisional candidate logic
The intended logic of A1 is:

> A local or mesoscopic environment should count as more pre-readably organized when its internal support structure is more coherent, less conflicted, and more mutually aligned than raw overlap alone would suggest, even though it may still remain below later readable-form thresholds.

In simpler terms:

- overlap says: relation is present
- A1 asks: does the local environment already hang together in a way that looks structured rather than merely present?

That is the conceptual difference.

---

## 8. Provisional procedural definition
A fully fixed formula is not claimed yet.
But A1 should already be defined procedurally enough for later first-pass work.

### Procedural sketch
For each local or mesoscopic unit:

1. define the local relational neighborhood
2. estimate internal support agreement across that neighborhood
3. penalize fragmentation, contradiction, or support-conflict
4. aggregate into one structured support-coherence marker

Possible ingredients:
- local support agreement fraction
- local conflict penalty
- neighborhood consistency score
- dispersion or incoherence penalty
- weighted local support concentration

### Minimal formal shell
A generic placeholder form could be:

`A1 = S_local - lambda * F_local`

where:
- `S_local` measures local support coherence
- `F_local` measures local fragmentation or conflict
- `lambda` sets the conflict penalty strength

This is a working shell, not yet a final formula.

---

## 9. Why A1 is not just overlap
A1 is **not** supposed to be just overlap because overlap alone says only:
- relation is present
- or local magnitude is strong

A1 instead asks:
- whether the local support environment is internally organized in a nontrivial way

So the intended distinction is:

- overlap = presence
- A1 = early organization by local support coherence

If A1 collapses into:
- smoothed overlap
- local density
- or simple magnitude averaging

then it has failed as a pre-readable organization marker.

---

## 10. Why A1 is not already readability
This section is crucial.

A1 is **not** supposed to be later readability because it should not require:

- visible later bridge form
- morphology-like articulation
- grid-side structure
- or already readable regime organization

Instead it should remain:
- a marker of deeper organization
- before readable articulation becomes strong enough to count as formed

If A1 only lights up once readable structure is already obvious, then it is not pre-readable.

---

## 11. Expected directional meaning
### Higher value means
Greater early structured support organization.
In the intended reading, a higher A1 means:
- the local environment is more internally coherent
- less fragmented
- and more plausibly organized before readability

### Lower value means
Weaker or less structured early organization.
In the intended reading, a lower A1 means:
- the local environment remains more fragmented, conflicted, or too weakly organized to count as pre-readable structure

If no clear direction survives implementation, A1 weakens substantially.

---

## 12. Main risks
### Main overlap-redundancy risk
A1 may reduce to a dressed-up local overlap average.

### Main readability-leakage risk
A1 may be defined too late and begin to track readable form rather than pre-readable organization.

### Main threshold/artifact risk
A1 may depend too strongly on neighborhood definition or support-coherence weighting.

### Main interpretability risk
Its agreement and conflict terms may become numerically active but structurally vague.

### Main current risk sentence
The main current risk of A1 is that it may look like early organization while in practice behaving either as smoothed overlap or as a weak early readability surrogate.

---

## 13. Relationship to the compatibility block
A1 is related to the compatibility block, but it is not identical to Candidate A there.

### Compatibility Candidate A asks
- does local support coherence predict later bridge-supportability relative to overlap and stabilization?

### Pre-readable Marker A1 asks
- is local support coherence already visible as structured organization before later readable articulation becomes strong?

So the overlap in spirit is real, but the target is different:

- compatibility Candidate A = selector-side
- pre-readable Marker A1 = early-organization-side

This distinction should remain explicit.

---

## 14. Relationship to the future readability boundary
A1 must later be compared not just against overlap-only, but against a readability-side boundary.

That future comparison should ask:

> Is A1 already clearly nontrivial while the readability-side marker is still weak, absent, or below threshold?

Only then would A1 count as evidence in favor of a genuinely pre-readable case.

So A1 is only one side of the future comparison frame.
