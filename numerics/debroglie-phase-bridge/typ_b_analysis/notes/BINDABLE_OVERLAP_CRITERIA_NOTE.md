# BINDABLE_OVERLAP_CRITERIA_NOTE

## Working criteria for bindable relational overlap

## 1. Purpose
This note sharpens the current internal concept of **bindable relational overlap** by asking a more operational question:

> **What would distinguish bindable overlap from generic correlation or generic overlap in the current project framework?**

The purpose is not yet to provide a final formal criterion, but to define a first disciplined working set of conditions that can guide future mechanistic thinking, testing, and model design.

This note follows the earlier concepts:
- **bound transition structure**
- **bound transition mode**
- **bindable relational overlap**
- **relational bonding architecture**

and tries to move one step closer to operational clarity.

---

## 2. Why this note is needed
The concept of bindable relational overlap is strong only if it can be separated from weaker notions such as:
- mere nearness
- generic correlation
- residual intermediate mixture
- or arbitrary bridge-like appearance

At the current stage, the project needs a sharper internal distinction between:

### 2.1 Generic correlation
There is some overlap or correlation, but it does not necessarily sustain a stable intermediate structure.

### 2.2 Bindable overlap
There is overlap of a kind that is compatible enough to support a coherent intermediate mode and a readable bound transition structure.

Without such a distinction, the term “bindable overlap” risks remaining only suggestive language.

---

## 3. First working definition
**Bindable relational overlap** shall currently mean:

A form of relational overlap that is not merely present, but sufficiently compatible, coherent, and structurally organized to support a stable intermediate mode from which a bound transition structure can emerge.

This already implies a separation between:
- overlap as availability
- and overlap as binding-capability

So the main question becomes:

> **What conditions turn overlap from merely available into genuinely bridge-supporting?**

---

## 4. Working distinction: generic correlation vs bindable overlap

## 4.1 Generic correlation
Generic correlation may include:
- measurable similarity
- measurable overlap
- partial adjacency in feature space
- weak or local intermediate behavior

But generic correlation may still fail to generate:
- persistence
- internal coherence
- structural legibility
- stable transition form

So generic correlation is not enough.

---

## 4.2 Bindable relational overlap
Bindable overlap should require more than correlation.  
At minimum, it should indicate:

1. **shared support**
   - there is a real intermediate overlap field, not a purely external linkage

2. **compatibility**
   - the overlap is not random or merely local, but jointly supportable as a stable mode

3. **coherence**
   - the structure does not dissolve under mild perturbation

4. **organized form**
   - the overlap condenses into a readable transition structure rather than featureless averaging

This is the current conceptual core.

---

## 5. Provisional criteria
A first internal working criterion set may therefore be:

## Criterion A — nontrivial overlap
There must be evidence of a real shared intermediate region rather than mere disjoint closeness.

Interpretation:
- overlap must not reduce to simple adjacency
- there must be some jointly occupied or jointly supportable transition zone

Question answered:
**Is there anything genuinely shared?**

---

## Criterion B — coherence / stability
The overlap must be stable enough to support persistence.

Current likely proxy:
- `simple_rigidity_surrogate`

Interpretation:
- the intermediate mode must not immediately dissolve
- some internal binding core is required

Question answered:
**Does the shared region hold together?**

---

## Criterion C — structured form
The overlap must acquire a readable organization rather than remain amorphous.

Current likely proxy:
- `grid_deviation_score`

Interpretation:
- bindable overlap should not appear merely as blurred averaging
- it should carry a structured transition morphology

Question answered:
**Does the shared region have an organized transition shape?**

---

## Criterion D — not reducible to simple distance
The overlap must not be explainable merely by proximity in a scalar structural coordinate.

Relevant current reminder:
- `distance_to_type_D` did not behave as internal primary carrier
- `spacing_cv` did not behave as internal primary carrier

Interpretation:
- simple nearness may accompany bridge-supporting overlap
- but should not be confused with the binding-capable part itself

Question answered:
**Is this more than simple closeness?**

---

## Criterion E — persistence under perturbation
A bindable overlap candidate should remain at least partially legible under perturbation, resampling, or reduced representation.

Interpretation:
- complete collapse under minimal disturbance suggests generic coincidence rather than binding capability
- partial retention under constrained conditions is more promising

Question answered:
**Is the intermediate mode robust enough to count as binding-capable?**

---

## 6. Current project mapping
Within the current A–E results, the most plausible provisional mapping is:

### Shared support field
- deep overlap/correlation layer
- Gram-like substrate
- relational field prior to regime readability

### Binding core
- `simple_rigidity_surrogate`

- `grid_deviation_score`

### Context / embedding quantities
- `distance_to_type_D`
- `spacing_cv`

This mapping supports the idea that:
- bindability is currently more plausibly tied to rigidity + structured form
- and less plausibly tied to distance/spacing alone

---

## 7. Minimal internal hypothesis
A first minimal mechanistic hypothesis could be:

> Overlap becomes bindable when it is not only present, but also coherent enough to sustain a shared mode and structured enough to generate a readable transition form.

In provisional project language:

- overlap available
- rigidity sufficiently high
- grid structure nontrivial
- then a bound transition mode becomes possible

This is not yet a final model, but it is a useful internal mechanistic sketch.

---

## 8. Current candidate signs of bindability
The current project suggests the following possible signs that overlap may be bindable rather than generic:

### 8.1 Strong internal anchor behavior
A quantity behaves as if removing it destroys the intermediate structure.

Observed candidate:
- `simple_rigidity_surrogate`

### 8.2 Necessary form-carrying behavior
A quantity behaves as if removing it destroys fuller structural readability.

Observed candidate:
- `grid_deviation_score`

### 8.3 Failure of pure-distance rescue
Distance-like quantities do not restore or carry the bridge by themselves.

Observed:
- `distance_to_type_D` not supported as internal primary carrier
- not supported as genuine H2 transfer stabilizer
- `spacing_cv` not supported as internal primary carrier

These observations are important because they constrain what bindability likely is **not**.

---

## 9. What bindability is likely not
At the current stage, bindable overlap is probably **not** best identified with:

- raw correlation magnitude alone
- scalar nearness alone
- generic intermediate appearance
- or any simple “between-ness” in feature space

This negative clarification is almost as important as the positive one.

---

## 10. Current best operational sentence
A useful present internal operational sentence is:

> **Bindable relational overlap is overlap that is coherent enough to hold, structured enough to form, and robust enough to remain readable as a bound intermediate mode.**

This sentence is still heuristic, but it is much closer to an operational criterion than the earlier looser language.

---

## 11. What could later become formalized
Later formal work might try to distinguish:

- overlap availability
- overlap compatibility
- overlap persistence
- overlap morphology
- and regime readability

This could lead to:
- explicit criterion sets
- threshold-free scores
- or a dynamical stability formulation

But at the current stage, the project is not yet there.

---

## 12. Immediate consequence for the project
The project should now avoid using “bindable overlap” as a decorative term.

Instead, whenever the term is used, it should imply at least the following internal checklist:

- Is there a genuinely shared intermediate region?
- Is it coherent?
- Is it morphologically organized?
- Is it more than distance-based closeness?
- Is it at least partly robust?

If several of these fail, “bindable” is probably too strong.

---

## 13. Bottom line
The current best internal reading is:

> **Bindable relational overlap** is not mere correlation and not mere nearness. It is overlap that is sufficiently coherent, structured, and robust to support a stable intermediate mode from which a bound transition structure can emerge. In the current project picture, this bindability appears more plausibly connected to rigidity plus structured form than to distance or spacing alone.

That is the present value of the concept at the criteria level.
