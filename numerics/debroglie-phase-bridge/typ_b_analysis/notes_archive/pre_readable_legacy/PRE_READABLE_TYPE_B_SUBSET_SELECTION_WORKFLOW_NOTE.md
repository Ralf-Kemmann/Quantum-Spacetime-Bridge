# PRE_READABLE_TYPE_B_SUBSET_SELECTION_WORKFLOW_NOTE

## Concrete internal selection workflow for the first bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transition subset inside Type-B

**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Status:** internal workflow note

---

## 1. Purpose
This note defines the concrete internal selection workflow for identifying the first actual subset to be used in the pre-readable Type-B pass.

Its purpose is to answer one practical question:

> **Given the target class “bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transition subset,” how do we actually choose one concrete internal Type-B subset in a disciplined, reproducible way?**

This note does not yet name the final subset.
It defines the workflow by which that subset should now be selected.

This is a workflow note, not a result note.

---

## 2. Why a selection workflow is needed
The pre-readable Type-B block now already has:

- an organization-side marker:
  - **A1 = structured support coherence**
- a readability-side boundary:
  - **`grid_deviation_score`**
- a threshold rule
- a comparison rule
- a first case-window definition
- a binding to an internal Type-B transitional zone
- and a first concrete segment-candidate class:
  - **bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transition subset**

What is still missing is the practical bridge from:
- target class
to
- actual chosen subset

Without this workflow, the subset choice remains too exposed to intuition-only selection.

So the workflow is now required.

---

## 3. Core selection philosophy
The workflow should obey one central principle:

> **Do not choose the strongest-looking or the most dramatic-looking region. Choose the earliest bridge-adjacent mesoscopic subset that is already more than background, while not yet clearly readability-side formed.**

This is the correct selection philosophy.

A shorter version is:

> **Earliest structured middle, not strongest final form.**

That principle should remain active through the whole workflow.

---

## 4. Overview of the workflow
The selection workflow should proceed through five steps:

1. define the candidate internal Type-B field of view
2. remove obvious no-go zones
3. identify bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transitional candidates
4. rank those candidates by pre-readable suitability
5. choose the narrowest interpretable candidate subset satisfying the rule

This is the full workflow in one line.

---

## 5. Step 1 — Define the candidate Type-B field of view
The workflow should begin by identifying the broad internal Type-B terrain within which subset selection is actually allowed.

This first field of view should include:
- internal Type-B regions plausibly relevant to emerging bridge-support organization

It should exclude:
- unrelated external background
- ring-specific boundary-case space
- and zones already known to belong to clearly different methodological blocks

The point of Step 1 is simply:
- define the map before choosing the subset

No subset selection should happen before this field is made explicit.

---

## 6. Step 2 — Remove the obvious no-go zones
Before looking for the target subset, exclude the zones that are clearly wrong for the first pass.

### Exclude Zone A — Weak background
Remove regions where:
- organization is too weak
- A1 is unlikely to become more than overlap-like
- and the pre-readable question would be too empty

### Exclude Zone B — Clearly readability-side formed regions
Remove regions where:
- later form/readability articulation already looks obviously strong
- `grid_deviation_score` is likely already above the formed-readability threshold
- or the case already reads as formed bridge structure

### Exclude Zone C — Highly anomalous local oddities
Remove regions that are:
- too exceptional
- too hard to reproduce
- or too idiosyncratic to function as first-pass calibration targets

After Step 2, what remains should be:
- plausible transition terrain only

---

## 7. Step 3 — Identify bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transitional candidates
Now scan the remaining terrain for subsets satisfying all of the following:

- bridge-adjacent rather than remote
- mesoscopic rather than point-like or global
- organization already plausibly above background
- readability not yet clearly locked in

At this stage, the goal is not to select one final subset yet.
The goal is to produce a short list of:
- **eligible candidate subsets**

These are the candidates that survive the first coarse filter.

---

## 8. Step 4 — Rank eligible subsets by pre-readable suitability
Once the candidate list exists, rank the candidates by the following ordered criteria.

### Criterion 1 — Early organization plausibility
How plausible is it that A1 could be materially active here?

### Criterion 2 — Readability still not locked in
How plausible is it that the readability boundary remains weak here?

### Criterion 3 — Bridge adjacency
How close is the subset to emerging bridge-support organization rather than diffuse background?

### Criterion 4 — Interpretability
How easy is it to describe and later review the subset as a coherent local or mesoscopic segment?

### Criterion 5 — Non-exoticness
How suitable is the subset as a first calibration target rather than an edge anomaly?

These criteria should be applied in this order.
The first-pass subset should favor:
- earlier bridge-adjacent organization with still-weak readability,
not
- late strong structure.

---

## 9. Step 5 — Choose the narrowest interpretable winning subset
After ranking, choose:

> **the narrowest interpretable bridge-adjacent mesoscopic subset that already looks more than background while still plausibly remaining below the readability-side threshold**

This is the final selection rule.

Why the narrowest?
Because the first pass should minimize regime mixing.

Why interpretable?
Because the subset must later support:
- A1 review
- readability-boundary review
- and a disciplined case record

So the winning subset should be:
- narrow
- bridge-adjacent
- transitional
- and explainable

---

## 10. What the workflow must not do
The workflow must not:

- choose the most globally impressive region
- choose the strongest structured region if it is already readability-side formed
- choose a subset simply because it confirms the desired story
- choose a region so broad that multiple regimes are mixed together
- or choose a region so tiny and odd that it cannot function as a clean first-pass case

These are all invalid selection moves.

---

## 11. Recommended practical review language during selection
A useful internal language for candidate screening is:

### Keep candidate if:
- already more than background
- still not clearly readability-side formed
- bridge-adjacent
- mesoscopic
- interpretable

### Reject candidate if:
- too weak
- too late
- too broad
- too exotic
- too hard to explain reproducibly

This language keeps the workflow disciplined.

---

## 12. Minimal output of the workflow
The workflow should minimally leave behind:

- one explicit broad Type-B field of view
- one list of excluded no-go zones
- one short list of eligible candidate subsets
- one ranking of those candidates
- one chosen subset for first-pass use

This is the minimum output bundle of the selection workflow.

---

## 13. Safe summary sentence
A useful internal sentence is:

> The first pre-readable Type-B subset should be selected by excluding weak background and already formed readability-side regions, then choosing the narrowest bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transition candidate in which A1 could plausibly be active while the readability boundary still remains weak.

A shorter version is:

> **Filter out background and formed zones, then choose the smallest bridge-adjacent transition subset that still looks organized but not yet readable.**

Both are good steering lines.

---

## 14. Bottom line
The concrete internal selection workflow for the first pre-readable Type-B subset is:

1. define the internal Type-B field of view
2. remove weak background, clearly formed readability-side regions, and exotic oddities
3. identify bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transition candidates
4. rank them by early-organization plausibility and still-weak readability
5. choose the narrowest interpretable winning subset

That is the current workflow standard for selecting the first actual pre-readable Type-B subset.
