# PRE_READABLE_TYPE_B_CANDIDATE_SUBSET_RANKING_TEMPLATE

## Template for ranking the eligible candidate subsets in the first pre-readable Type-B pass

**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Status:** internal ranking template

---

## 1. Purpose
This template is used to rank the eligible candidate subsets after the field of view has been fixed, the no-go zones have been excluded, and the eligible subset list has been created.

Its purpose is to answer one practical question:

> **Which eligible internal Type-B subset is the strongest first-pass candidate for the pre-readable comparison, and why?**

This is a ranking template, not a result note.

---

## 2. Why this template is needed
The pre-readable Type-B block now already has:

- a field of view
- explicit no-go zones
- an eligible candidate subset list
- a subset-selection workflow
- and a subset-selection record template

What is still needed is the bridge between:

- **eligibility**
and
- **final choice**

That bridge is:
- **ranking**

Without an explicit ranking step, the jump from eligible list to chosen subset remains too vulnerable to intuition-only selection.

This template prevents that.

---

## 3. Core ranking principle
The ranking should obey one central principle:

> **Do not rank by strongest-looking final structure. Rank by the best bridge-adjacent mesoscopic transition candidate in which early organization is already plausible while readability is still not yet clearly locked in.**

A shorter version is:

> **Rank the earliest structured middle, not the strongest final form.**

This is the governing ranking principle.

---

## 4. Ranking criteria
The eligible candidate subsets should be ranked using the following ordered criteria.

### Criterion 1 — Early-organization plausibility
How plausible is it that A1 could be materially active in this subset?

### Criterion 2 — Readability still not locked in
How plausible is it that the readability boundary remains still weak here?

### Criterion 3 — Bridge adjacency
How close is the subset to emerging bridge-support structure rather than diffuse background?

### Criterion 4 — Interpretability
How easy is the subset to describe, delimit, and later review as one coherent case?

### Criterion 5 — Non-exoticness
How suitable is the subset as a first-pass calibration target rather than an idiosyncratic anomaly?

These criteria should be applied in this order.

## 8. Recommended ranking rule
The first-pass winner should not be chosen as:

- the strongest globally structured subset
- the most impressive-looking subset
- or the subset with the highest late readability

Instead, the winner should be chosen as:

> **the narrowest interpretable eligible subset that best satisfies early-organization plausibility while still remaining below clear readability lock-in**

This is the correct ranking rule.

---

## 9. Tie-breaking rule
If two candidate subsets look similar in overall strength, use the following tie-break order:

### Tie-break 1
Prefer the subset where readability still looks less locked in.

### Tie-break 2
If still tied, prefer the subset that is more bridge-adjacent.

### Tie-break 3
If still tied, prefer the subset that is narrower and easier to interpret.

This tie-break rule prevents the ranking from drifting toward:
- later stronger-looking but already too formed subsets

That is important.

---

## 10. What this ranking must not do
The ranking must not:

- promote the strongest final-looking subset automatically
- silently reintroduce excluded no-go zones
- rank by aesthetic appeal
- blur broad mixed subsets into “probably best”
- or treat late readability strength as if it were a virtue for the pre-readable first pass

These are invalid ranking habits.

---

## 11. Winning-subset transition rule
At the end of ranking, exactly one subset should move into the winning-subset block.

### Transition rule
The subset transferred as the winner should be:
- highest-ranked overall
- still plausibly below readability lock-in
- bridge-adjacent
- mesoscopic
- and the narrowest interpretable acceptable candidate

This transition must be explicit.

---

## 12. Safe summary sentence
A useful internal sentence is:

> The first pre-readable Type-B subset should be chosen by ranking eligible candidates according to how plausibly they already support early organization while still remaining below clear readability lock-in, not according to how strongly formed they already look.

A shorter version is:

> **Rank by early organization under still-weak readability, not by strongest final form.**

Both are good steering lines.

---

## 13. Bottom line
The candidate-subset ranking step for the first pre-readable Type-B pass should now be recorded using:

- ordered ranking criteria
- one compact ranking table
- one explicit tie-break rule
- and one explicit winner-transition rule

This template defines how that ranking should be documented.
