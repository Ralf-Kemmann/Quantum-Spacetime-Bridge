# COMPATIBILITY_FIRST_PASS_UPDATED_OUTCOME_AND_PROMOTION_NOTE

## Updated global outcome language and promotion language after the first qualitative compatibility content readout

**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Status:** internal update note

---

## 1. Purpose
This note updates two things in light of the first qualitative content readout:

- the **global first-pass outcome language**
- the **promotion language**

Its purpose is to answer one practical question:

> **Given that the compatibility block now has a first substantive qualitative A1/B1 readout, how should the global outcome wording and promotion wording now be stated without overclaiming?**

This is an update note, not a final result note.

---

## 2. Why this update is needed
The compatibility block has now moved beyond:

- empty placeholders
- and purely structural admissibility language

It now has a first substantive qualitative content reading:

- **A1** = weak but qualitatively plausible early-support-coherence signal
- **B1** = weak but qualitatively plausible early-fragility signal, with higher interpretation risk

and the first joint asymmetry:

> **A1 currently leads qualitatively; B1 remains in play but less securely.**

That means the older global wording:

- “explicitly in play, not yet evidentially decided”

is still not wrong,
but it is now too weak.

The outcome language should now reflect the fact that:
- a first qualitative front-runner exists

without pretending that:
- the first pass is already evidentially closed.

---

## 3. Governing update principle
The correct update must stay between two errors.

### Error 1 — Too weak
Pretending the block is still only blankly open, even though a first real qualitative asymmetry now exists.

### Error 2 — Too strong
Pretending that the first pass has already delivered a final evidential winner.

So the governing principle is:

> **The updated wording should explicitly acknowledge that A1 is now the qualitative front-runner, while still keeping both candidates below formal evidential promotion.**

A shorter version is:

> **A1 leads qualitatively, but no formal win yet.**

That is the governing principle.

---

## 4. Updated global first-pass outcome language

### Recommended full wording
> **Updated global first-pass outcome: both Candidate A1 and Candidate B1 remain in play within the accepted compatibility frame, but the first qualitative content readout now identifies A1 as the cleaner and more plausible first-pass lead, while B1 remains weaker and more interpretation-fragile. No final evidential first-pass verdict is yet justified.**

This is the preferred full wording.

### Recommended compact wording
> **Global first-pass state: A1 currently leads qualitatively; B1 remains in play but less securely; no final evidential verdict yet.**

This is the preferred compact wording.

---

## 5. Why this updated outcome wording is correct
This wording is correct because it captures all of the current methodological truth at once.

### What is now true
- A1 is no longer just structurally admissible
- B1 is no longer just structurally admissible
- both candidates have qualitative content language
- a first asymmetry between the candidates is now explicit
- A1 currently reads as the cleaner first-pass lead

### What is still not true
- A1 has not yet been shown materially active by concrete run-level content
- B1 has not yet been excluded by concrete run-level content
- neither candidate has yet earned a final run-level category
- the selector problem is not solved
- and the first pass is not evidentially closed

That is exactly why this updated outcome wording is the correct one.

---

## 6. Relationship to the formal first-pass outcome categories
The formal run-level categories remain:

- **Outcome A — No useful first-pass candidate**
- **Outcome B — Weak but non-tautological candidate found**
- **Outcome C — Promising pre-stabilization candidate found**

The present updated wording is still:
- not yet one of those final categories in strict evidential form

But it now sits closer to Outcome B than before, because the block has moved from:
- blank openness

to:
- first qualitative candidate differentiation

A careful way to say this is:

> **The block has not yet reached a formal run-level category assignment, but its current qualitative state is now nearer to a weak-candidate reading than to complete undecided blankness.**

This is the right disciplined reading.

## 7. Updated promotion language

### Recommended full wording
> **Updated promotion language: no candidate is formally promoted yet, but A1 is now the current qualitative front-runner for the next evidential sharpening step, while B1 remains retained as a secondary candidate under higher interpretation risk.**

This is the preferred full wording.

### Recommended compact wording
> **Promotion status: still deferred, but A1 is the current lead candidate for first evidential sharpening.**

This is the preferred compact wording.

---

## 8. Why this updated promotion wording is correct
This wording is correct because it preserves the distinction between:

- **front-runner**
and
- **promoted candidate**

A1 may now be called:
- the current qualitative lead

But A1 may not yet be called:
- formally promoted by evidence

B1 may still be kept:
- in play

But B1 should now be read:
- as the riskier secondary candidate, not as an equal qualitative lead

This is the correct promotion-language balance.

---

## 9. What must still not be claimed
Even after this update, the compatibility block is still not allowed to claim:

- A1 has won evidentially
- B1 has been ruled out evidentially
- Outcome B or C has been formally secured
- the selector layer has been found
- or promotion has already occurred in the strong sense

The update strengthens the language,
but it does not close the pass.

This must remain explicit.
