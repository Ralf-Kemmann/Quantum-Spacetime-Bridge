# BRIDGE_CARRIER_SYNTHESIS_NOTE_E  
## Internal addendum after Block E  
### H2 discrimination test for `distance_to_type_D` as possible transfer stabilizer

## 1. Purpose of this addendum
This addendum extends the current bridge-carrier synthesis after Blocks A–D by documenting the result of **Block E**, whose purpose was to test a narrower question that emerged from the earlier sequence:

> **Is `distance_to_type_D` a genuine reduced-H2 transfer stabilizer, or was its earlier weak H2 appearance only a mirror-specific artifact of reduced proxy-based transfer?**

This question became necessary because the A–D sequence had already suggested a split between:

- **internal carrier anatomy**, and
- **reduced transfer anatomy**.

In particular, the earlier reduced H2 mirror had produced a weak residual signal in variants that retained `distance_to_type_D`, even though that variable had not appeared as an internal primary carrier.

---

## 2. Starting point from Blocks A–D
Before Block E, the internally strongest reading was:

- `simple_rigidity_surrogate` acts as the strongest **internal anchor**
- `grid_deviation_score` acts as a **necessary internal co-carrier**
- `distance_to_type_D` and `spacing_cv` appear **removable internally**
- reduced H2 proxy-transfer did **not** reproduce the internal core directly
- a weak residual H2 signal had appeared in some variants retaining `distance_to_type_D`

That earlier reduced H2 appearance was not yet sufficient to justify a transfer-stabilizer claim. It only justified opening the question in a more discriminating way.

---

## 3. Why Block E was needed
Block E was designed specifically to avoid a premature overreading of the earlier H2 residual effect.

The narrower logic was:

- if `distance_to_type_D` is a **genuine transfer stabilizer**, then adding it to the reduced proxy-rigidity H2 setting should improve H2 behavior in a specific and reproducible way
- if the earlier effect was only a **mirror artifact** or generic reduced-set fluctuation, then `distance_to_type_D` should fail to improve H2 behavior once directly compared against nearby alternatives

---

## 4. Block E design
Block E tested the following reduced H2 discrimination variants:

- `full_reference`
- `proxy_rigidity_only`
- `proxy_rigidity_plus_distance`
- `proxy_rigidity_plus_grid`
- `proxy_rigidity_plus_spacing`
- `proxy_rigidity_plus_grid_plus_distance`

The logic was intentionally narrow:

- `proxy_rigidity_only` served as the minimal reduced-H2 baseline
- `+ distance` tested the hypothesized distance-stabilizer effect directly
- `+ grid` tested whether structural co-carrier information is the more relevant reduced-H2 support factor
- `+ spacing` served as a nearby comparison quantity
- `+ grid + distance` tested whether distance only helps inside a richer reduced core rather than alone

---

## 5. Main result of Block E
The Block E result does **not** support `distance_to_type_D` as a genuine reduced-H2 transfer stabilizer.

### 5.1 Reduced H2 partial signals
The only weak partial reduced-H2 signals appeared for:

- `proxy_rigidity_only`
- `proxy_rigidity_plus_grid`

Among these, the strongest partial reduced-H2 behavior appeared for:

- `proxy_rigidity_plus_grid`

### 5.2 Distance-specific comparison
By contrast:

- `proxy_rigidity_plus_distance` did **not** improve reduced H2 behavior over `proxy_rigidity_only`
- `proxy_rigidity_plus_grid_plus_distance` did **not** improve over `proxy_rigidity_plus_grid`
- in the tested combinations, adding `distance_to_type_D` did **not** preserve or strengthen the partial reduced-H2 signal

This is the key discrimination result.

---

## 6. Immediate interpretation
The most defensible current reading is therefore:

> Block E does **not** support `distance_to_type_D` as a genuine reduced-H2 transfer stabilizer.

More precisely:

- the earlier weak H2 appearance of distance-retaining variants was **not** reproduced in the stricter discrimination setting
- the reduced-H2 side responds more plausibly to the combination of **proxy-rigidity plus grid structure**
- `distance_to_type_D` does not show the expected stabilizing behavior once tested against nearby reduced-set alternatives

---

## 7. What Block E positively suggests instead
Although Block E mainly ruled out one suspicion, it also sharpened the positive reading.

The strongest weak reduced-H2 partial signal appears not for distance, but for:

- **proxy-rigidity plus grid**

This is important because it preserves a certain continuity with the internal A–D picture:

### Internal picture
- rigidity = anchor
- grid = necessary co-carrier

### Reduced H2 picture
- rigidity alone gives at most a very weak residual trace
- rigidity plus grid gives the strongest partial reduced-H2 signal currently visible
- distance does not improve this partial reduced-H2 retention

---

## 8. Revised role of `distance_to_type_D`
After Block E, the role of `distance_to_type_D` should be revised downward.

### What is no longer supported
The project should **not** currently claim that:

- `distance_to_type_D` is a confirmed reduced-H2 transfer stabilizer
- distance provides the main bridge-retention channel under incomplete H2 mapping
- or the weak earlier H2 signal was specifically distance-driven

### What may still remain true
It may still be true that `distance_to_type_D` is useful in other contexts:

- as a descriptive internal reference quantity
- as a structural comparison axis
- or as a secondary organizing quantity

But under the present reduced-H2 discrimination test, it does **not** behave like the decisive reduced-transfer stabilizer that had briefly seemed possible.

---

---

## 10. Current best synthetic reading after A–E
The strongest defensible synthesis now becomes:

> The bridge is currently best read as a rigidity-centered internal structure with grid deviation as a necessary co-carrier of the fuller internal bridge picture. Under reduced proxy-mediated H2 transfer, this internal core does not reproduce fully. The best weak residual H2 signal is currently associated with proxy-rigidity plus grid, whereas `distance_to_type_D` is not supported as a genuine transfer stabilizer by the Block E discrimination test.

---

## 11. What Block E has achieved
Even though Block E did not produce a positive transfer result, it achieved something scientifically valuable.

### 11.1 It reduced ambiguity
The project no longer has to keep the distance-stabilizer idea in a half-supported limbo.  
That idea has now been explicitly tested and is presently **not supported**.

### 11.2 It strengthened the bridge anatomy
The bridge anatomy now looks less diffuse:

- internal anchor = rigidity
- internal fuller-form co-carrier = grid
- reduced-H2 weak retention = again closest to rigidity-plus-grid, not distance

### 11.3 It clarified the next work direction
The project can now stop spending conceptual energy on a weakly supported distance-transfer rescue hypothesis and focus instead on the more interesting remaining question:

> Why does rigidity-plus-grid retain only weak residual H2 structure, and what exactly is lost between internal carrier anatomy and reduced proxy-mediated transfer?

---

## 12. What this still does **not** justify
Even after Block E, caution remains necessary.

The current results do **not** justify the claim that:

- proxy-rigidity plus grid is already a successful transfer core,
- reduced H2 behavior is now fully explained,
- the current corridor-based evaluator is the final project-level decision stack,
- or the full physical mechanism of bridge transport has already been isolated.

The present status remains:

- internal,
- evaluator-dependent,
- reduced-H2-specific,
- and still part of a staged isolation program.

---

## 13. Immediate consequence for next work
The next step should now be shaped by the cleaner post-E picture.

### Preserve the distinction
The project should continue to keep separate:

- internal carrier role
- reduced transfer retention role
- diagnostic/reporting logic

### Narrow the next question
The next sharp question is no longer:

> Does distance help transfer?

but rather:

> Why does proxy-rigidity plus grid produce only weak reduced-H2 retention, and what additional ingredient is missing between the internal bridge core and reduced H2 transport?

That is a far more grounded next target.

---

The cleanest current synthesis after Block E is:

> **Internally**, the bridge remains best described as a rigidity-centered structure, with `simple_rigidity_surrogate` as the internal anchor and `grid_deviation_score` as a necessary co-carrier for the fuller bridge picture.  
> **Under reduced H2 proxy-mediated transfer**, the internal core does not reproduce fully. The strongest weak residual H2 signal is associated with proxy-rigidity plus grid, while `distance_to_type_D` is **not supported** as a genuine reduced-H2 transfer stabilizer by the Block E discrimination test.

That is the current best internal reading after A–E.
